How Corona Spreads

Respiratory Droplets

Virus spreads primarily through droplets of saliva or discharge from the nose when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Human Contact

People can catch COVID-19 if they breathe in droplets from a person with COVID-19 who coughs out or exhales droplets

Contaminated Objects

People can catch COVID-19 by touching contaminated objects or surfaces, then touching their eyes, nose or mouth.

Symptoms of Covid-19

The most common symptoms of COVID-19 are fever, tiredness, and dry cough. Some patients may have aches and pains, nasal congestion, runny nose, sore throat or diarrhea. These symptoms are usually mild and begin gradually. Around 1 out of every 6 people who gets COVID-19 becomes seriously ill and develops difficulty breathing. Older people, and those with underlying medical problems like high blood pressure, heart problems or diabetes, are more likely to develop serious illness. People with fever, cough and difficulty breathing should seek medical attention.

The Coronavirus Explained

Take steps to protect yourself

Wash Your Hands

Wash your hands regularly with soap and water, or clean them with alcohol-based hand rub.

Social Distancing

Maintain at least 1 metre distance between you and people coughing or sneezing.

Disinfect Often Touched Surfaces

Clean the area or item with soap and water or another detergent if it is dirty. Then, use a household disinfectant.

Take steps to protect others

Stay Home

Stay home if you feel unwell. If you have a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention and call in advance. Follow the directions of your local health authority.

Cover Coughs and Sneezes

Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when you cough or sneeze or use the inside of your elbow.
